Council Members


In October of 2013 the leaders of the Princeton Gates-Millenium Scholars, Princeton LEDA Scholars, and Princeton University Quest Scholars Network came together and founded the Princeton Hidden Minority Council with the goal of building the infrastructure for a thriving first generation community at Princeton.

The leaders below were not satisfied by the silence that existed regarding socio-economic background and experience on Princeton's campus; acceptance comes out of understanding, and understanding comes out of education. We are committed to serving as representatives for the first generation and low-income community along with our peers, and we hope that you empathize with our experiences or better understand them and that you reach out to us about anything, like questions regarding our majors or the activities we are involved with!

To read the Council Member's narrative, click their picture to be taken to their bio.

Co-Chair

The Princeton Hidden Minority Council would like to welcome you to our website, home to the Princeton community of first generation and low-income students, faculty, and administration. Here you’ll find resources, blogs, and student narratives written for and by this community.

Co-Chair

Together we stand proud of the lives that have shaped our perspective; but together we also acknowledge the unique challenges that we face as first generation and low income community members.

Treasurer

Please use this site as a resource, to connect with other individuals in the first generation and low income community, to look for more resources to support you during your time at Princeton, or to just learn through the narratives and blogs what being a first generation or low-income community member is like.

Secretary

We encourage you to get involved in our community and are excited to have you join us!

- The Princeton Hidden Minority Council
